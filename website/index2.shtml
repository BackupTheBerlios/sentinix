2003-11-27: Misconfiguration notice!

In SENTINIX 0.70.5 and Beta 01 Cacti is configured with UCD-SNMP 4.X which doesn't work, it should be NET-SNMP 5.x, which works fine. Change "Cacti settings", "General", "SNMP Version" to "NET-SNMP 5.x" and save.

WHAT IS SENTINIX?

SENTINIX is a GNU/Linux distribution designed for monitoring, auditing, intrusion detection, statistics/graphing and anti-spam. It's completely free; free to use, free to modify and free to distribute. SENTINIX includes the following software, installed and pre-configured; Nagios, Nagat, Snort, SnortCenter, ACID, Cacti, RRDTool, Nessus, Postfix, MailScanner, SpamAssassin, openMosix, MySQL, Apache, PHP, Perl, Python and lots more.

SENTINIX is shipped with a lot of programs, not all licensed under the same license (e.g. GNU GPL). SENTINIX contains (and will only contain) software whose license has been approved by the Open Source Initiative.

Compledge Sentinel was the predecessor of SENTINIX. The website of Sentinel (http://compledge.com/sentinel) has been closed down due to a transfer of ISP. The old Compledge Sentinel ISO-image is available at ftp://kaosphere.ath.cx/sentinel.

SENTINIX NEED DEVELOPERS!

Are you a GNU/Linux developer? Know anything about what makes a GNU/Linux distribution work? Want to help out?

SENTINIX is a very ambitious project, aiming at being the best security solution on the market, whether compared to free or commercial products. SENTINIX will always be free, that is, freely distributable and only contain OSI approved software; free to modify, free to use, free to distribute and free of charge!

What's in it for SENTINIX developers?

Despite the lack of monitary award, there are other benefits. Since SENTINIX is a specialists' GNU/Linux distribution designed for critical tasks, helping to develop SENTINIX could boost your career, even bring in some salery from deploying SENTINIX at, e.g. companies that would want a monitoring and/or anti-spam solution. In the future, there will be support organizations surrounding the SENTINIX GNU/Linux distribution and these organizations will make money by selling support agreements and consultation. Support and consultation is easier to sell if the one selling it also helps to develop the software.
